The History of Pancakes

Pancakes are one of the oldest dishes known to humanity. It is believed that the first pancakes appeared in Russia in the 9th century. This dish was associated with pagan traditions and symbolized the sun. Pancakes were served during Maslenitsa as a symbol of saying goodbye to winter and welcoming spring.

Over time, pancake recipes spread around the world, and now every country has its own variations. However, the classic Russian milk pancakes remain the most famous.

Recipe and Traditions

Every nation has its rich ancient traditions, which are present in clothing, everyday life, language, songs, and food. Ukrainian traditions in cooking various national dishes are especially interesting. For every major holiday, Ukrainians have a list of obligatory dishes. Can you imagine Christmas without kutya or Easter without paska?

Milk pancakes are considered traditional Ukrainian food. There are many pancake recipes, but the main ingredients always include milk, sugar, salt, eggs, and butter. The flour used can vary, and so can the fillings. This is where the creativity of the cook comes into play. Who wouldn't want to enjoy pancakes with honey, apples, soft cheese, condensed milk, mushrooms, or meat?

Useful Tips for Making Pancakes

When making pancakes, cooks often face challenges: how to make them thin and sturdy, or fluffy and light. Here are a few secrets:

  • Wheat flour is best for making pancakes, but if you prefer fluffier pancakes, you can use oat or buckwheat flour.
  • To avoid lumps, add flour in small portions, constantly stirring.
  • For extra tender pancakes, sift the flour 2â€“3 times.
  • Water is the base for thin and sturdy pancakes, but those made with milk are much tastier. Mix milk and water for both sturdy and delicious pancakes.
  • If you use kefir to make pancakes, they will be fluffy. The same effect is achieved with yeast dough. It is advisable to have a separate frying pan for making pancakes.
